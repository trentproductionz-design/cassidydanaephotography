Featured Story

The Art of Being Present

How documenting love stories across continents taught me that the best photographs happen when couples forget the camera exists.

A couple shares an intimate moment aboard a traditional boat in Santorini. Photo: Cassidy Danae, 2024

Photography chose me long before I chose it. Growing up in Michigan, I was the kid always carrying a disposable camera, documenting everything from family dinners to the changing seasons along Lake Michigan. But it wasn't until I photographed my first wedding that I understood what I was really meant to capture: not just images, but the feeling of a moment.

Today, my work takes me from the volcanic cliffs of Santorini to intimate barn ceremonies in the Midwest. Each destination brings its own challenges and magic, but the core of what I do remains unchanged. I'm there to document the authentic moments—the nervous laughter before walking down the aisle, the way your partner looks at you when they think no one's watching, the tears your father tries to hide during his speech.

The couples I work with aren't looking for posed perfection. They want their real story told. They want to remember how it felt, not just how it looked. That's why I approach each wedding as a journalist would approach a story—observing, anticipating, being present but never intrusive. My camera becomes invisible, and what remains is pure, unfiltered emotion.

I'm not just capturing moments. I'm preserving the feeling of the day.

Profile

Meet Your Third Wheel

I'm the gal with the camera, your dance floor pal, the tissue holder, finder of the hidden bustle buttons, and the one saying "everything is FINEEE" when your day is running slightly behind schedule. Believe me, we find and make up time elsewhere. I. GOT. YOU.

When I'm not photographing love stories around the world, you'll find me obsessed with a good grilled cheese, wrapped in cozy blankets with Ben & Jerry's, binging The Office for the hundredth time, or planning my next travel adventure with my fur-babes: Stella, Beau, Hammy, and Rosie.

But mostly? I'm here to capture the real, unscripted moments—the ones you'll want to remember forever. The belly laughs, the happy tears, the way your partner looks at you when they think no one's watching. Those are the moments that matter.

My approach is simple: I treat your wedding day like the important piece of history it is. I'm part photojournalist, part hype person, and entirely devoted to making sure you feel comfortable, supported, and completely yourself from start to finish.

— Cass
